# Quotable Quotes from $100M Leads
**Author:** Alex Hormozi
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-10

## On Volume and Persistence

> "Advertising always works, it's only a matter of efficiency. So once you start, keep going until it works."

> "Most people dramatically underestimate the volume it takes to make advertising work. They're not doing half as much or a third as much as is required. In fact, they're doing dramatically less."

> "I was doing 1/1500th the level of effort required to make a flyer campaign work -- I just didn't know it."

> "If you do 100 primary actions every day for 100 straight days, you will not be in the same place."

> "100 per day, not 100 over time."

> "You either win or you learn."

> "You cannot lose if you do not quit."

> "Every die hits its green streak when rolled enough times."

> "People try shortcuts for a decade until they realize they should have picked a strategy and stuck with it for a decade."

> "Do more than they do, and you will have more than they have."

## On Strategy and Frameworks

> "There have only ever been four ways to let other people know about the stuff you sell."

> "More, better, new. In that order. Always."

> "Find the constraint. It's where the biggest drop-off happens. Fix that and only that."

> "Give away the secrets, sell the implementation."

> "Give give give give give give give ask. That's the cadence."

> "You don't just commit to doing something a specific number of times...you commit to the work until you hit a specific number of outcomes--no matter what."

## On Value and Goodwill

> "If you make more value than you charge, people tell their friends."

> "Goodwill is the gap between what you charge and what you deliver. The bigger the gap, the more they tell others."

> "There are two ways to create a compounding business. You can find more people that never stop buying your stuff or you can find more people who never stop selling it for you."

## On Lead Generation Tactics

> "ACA stands for Acknowledge, Compliment, Ask. In that order."

> "Every unit of content, no matter how long or short, has three elements: Hook, Retain, Reward."

> "Every ad has three parts: a callout, value, and a call to action."

> "Personalization is the key to cold outreach... Big fast value is the other key."

> "The nine-word email. Nine words. That's it."

> "Document, demonstrate, duplicate. In that order."

> "Whisper, tease, shout. That's how you launch affiliates."

## On Mindset and Business Building

> "The last skill you ever need to learn is how to get other people to do everything you need for you."

> "Whether it's 1%, 5%, or 10%, set some percentage of your advertising budget aside to try new things without expecting a return. Consider it an investment in your education."

> "I expect to crack a new lead source in three to six months (and this isn't my first rodeo). So if your expectations are faster than that, do you think your expectations are reasonable?"

> "Success comes down to doing the obvious thing for an uncommonly long period of time without convincing yourself you're smarter than you are." -- Neil Strauss (quoted by Hormozi)

> "Your speed to making big money depends on how fast you learn the skills to making big money."

> "You can either do the advertising or other people can. And there are more 'other people' than there are of you."

## On Scaling

> "Client financed acquisition means you break even on customer acquisition within 30 days."

> "LTGP to CAC. Three to one or better."

> "Like referrals, affiliates aren't an advertising method you can 'do.' They're people who advertise your stuff to benefit you both."
