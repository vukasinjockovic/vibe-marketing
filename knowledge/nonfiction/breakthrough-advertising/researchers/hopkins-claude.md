**Status:** stub

## Summary
Claude Hopkins was an early 20th-century advertising pioneer referenced by Schwartz as the figure who lifted desire-based copy techniques out of patent-medicine advertising and established them in every category of general advertising, before the First World War. Hopkins is considered a foundational figure in direct-response and scientific advertising.

## Key Findings / What He Contributed
1. Lifted desire-amplification copy techniques from patent-medicine into general advertising before World War I.
   -- Source: *Breakthrough Advertising*, Ch. 8 (~line 8335)

## Key Quotes
> (Schwartz on Hopkins): "Before the First World War, Claude Hopkins had lifted them out of patent-medicine and established them in every category of general advertising."
