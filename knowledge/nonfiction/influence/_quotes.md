# Influence, New and Expanded -- Key Quotes
Source: Robert Cialdini, "Influence, New and Expanded" (2021)

---

## On Automatic Behavior

> "Click, and the appropriate program is activated; run, and out rolls the standard sequence of behaviors."
-- Chapter 1

> "You and I exist in an extraordinarily complicated environment, easily the most rapidly moving and complex ever on this planet."
-- Chapter 1

> "Civilization advances by extending the number of operations we can perform without thinking about them."
-- Alfred North Whitehead, epigraph to Chapter 1

## On Reciprocity

> "The rule says that we should try to repay what another person has provided us."
-- Chapter 2

> "There's nothing more expensive than that which comes for free."
-- Japanese proverb, cited in Chapter 2

> "Favors are to be met with favors; it does not require that tricks be met with favors."
-- Chapter 2

> "The truly gifted negotiator is one whose initial position is exaggerated just enough to allow for a series of small reciprocal concessions."
-- Chapter 2

> "Problem-free may not feel as good to people as problem-freed."
-- Chapter 2

## On Liking

> "There is nothing more effective in selling anything than getting customers to believe, really believe, you like them."
-- Joe Girard, cited in Chapter 3

> "We like people who are like us."
-- Chapter 3

> "People just don't sue the doctors they like."
-- Chapter 3

## On Social Proof

> "Since 95 percent of the people are imitators and only 5 percent initiators, people are persuaded more by the actions of others than by any proof we can offer."
-- Chapter 4

> "We seem to assume that if a lot of people are doing the same thing, they must know something we don't."
-- Chapter 4

> "Popularity precipitates popularity."
-- Chapter 4 (on Netflix revealing viewing data)

## On Authority

> "Follow an expert."
-- Virgil, epigraph to Chapter 5

> "It is the extreme willingness of adults to go to almost any lengths on the command of an authority that constitutes the chief finding of the study."
-- Stanley Milgram, cited in Chapter 5

> "Audiences trust and follow the advice of a set of experts more than that of any one of them."
-- Chapter 5

## On Scarcity

> "The way to love anything is to realize that it might be lost."
-- G.K. Chesterton, cited in Chapter 6

> "People assign more value to opportunities that are less available."
-- Chapter 6

> "Panicky, feverish reactions have no place in wise compliance decisions."
-- Chapter 6

## On Commitment/Consistency

> "After making a commitment (that is, taking an action, stand, or position), people are more willing to agree to requests in keeping with the prior commitment."
-- Chapter 7

> "Commitments are most effective when they are active, public, effortful, and viewed as internally motivated."
-- Chapter 7

> "Commitment decisions, even erroneous ones, have a tendency to be self-perpetuating."
-- Chapter 7

## On Unity

> "People say yes to someone they consider one of them."
-- Chapter 8

> "The experience of 'we'-ness (unity) with others is about shared identities -- tribe-like categories that individuals use to define themselves and their groups."
-- Chapter 8

## On Ethical Practice

> "The mimics are undermining our confidence in the worth of the shortcut information we seek."
-- Chapter 1 (on fake social proof, manufactured authority, etc.)

> "Near-perfect ratings undermine trustworthiness."
-- Spiegel Research Center, cited in Chapter 5 (optimal rating is 4.2-4.7 stars)
