# Amos Tversky
**Type:** researcher
**Status:** partial
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-10

## Summary
Amos Tversky was an Israeli cognitive and mathematical psychologist who, with Daniel Kahneman, developed Prospect Theory and identified key cognitive biases. In Never Split the Difference, Tversky is referenced as co-developer of the research that underpins Voss's loss framing strategies. Tversky died in 1996 before the Nobel Prize was awarded to Kahneman in 2002 (the Nobel is not awarded posthumously).

## Key Findings
1. Co-developed Prospect Theory with Kahneman, establishing that people evaluate outcomes relative to reference points and are loss-averse.
   -- Source: Never Split the Difference, Ch.6 (referenced)
