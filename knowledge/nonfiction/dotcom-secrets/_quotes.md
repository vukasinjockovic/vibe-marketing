# Key Quotes from DotCom Secrets
**Source:** DotCom Secrets by Russell Brunson (2015)

---

## On the Core Problem
> "More often than not, it's a FUNNEL problem."

> "The problem is you can't spend enough to acquire a customer, and the way to fix that problem is to fix your sales funnel."

## On Spending to Win
> "Ultimately, the business that can spend the most to acquire a customer wins." -- Dan Kennedy (cited by Brunson)

## On Building a List
> "Russell, you have to build a list." -- Mark Joyner (cited by Brunson)

> "Your one and ONLY goal is to OWN all the traffic you can."

## On Traffic
> "Not all clicks are created equal." -- Mark Joyner (cited by Brunson)

> "The frame people enter your website through is probably the most important thing you can know." -- Mark Joyner

## On the Value Ladder
> "There's no end to your Value Ladder."

> "Russell, that's a five thousand dollar buyer lead -- you need to sell them something else!"

> "A percentage of your audience will always want to pay you the premium to get more value."

## On Free
> "There was power in free!" -- Brunson, citing Dan Ariely's research

## On the Attractive Character
> "Creating your Attractive Character can mean the difference between making one thousand dollars a month and one hundred thousand."

> "No one wants to hear about the perfect person -- because you can't relate."

> "Being neutral is boring."

## On Email
> "My emails switched from 100% content to 90% entertainment and just 10% content, and my readership, opens, clicks, and sales all skyrocketed."

> "EVERY EMAIL and each story must be tied back into some type of offer for your audience."

> "If I don't email my list every day, I lose money every day."

## On Buyers
> "A buyer is a buyer is a buyer." -- Dan Kennedy (cited by Brunson)

> "Once you get someone to say the first yes, it's so much easier to get the second yes."

## On The Stack
> "The only thing your prospect remembers when you sell is the last thing you showed him." -- Armand Morin (cited by Brunson)

## On Continuity
> "If you don't have continuity, then you don't have a business." -- David Frey (cited by Brunson)

## On Modeling
> "If you want to be successful in any part of your life, you needed to find someone else who is already doing what you want to do and model your efforts after theirs." -- Tony Robbins (cited by Brunson)

> "Do NOT try to re-invent the wheel."

## On Pre-Framing
> "It's all about how I introduce you." -- Armand Morin

## On the OTO
> "Congratulations, your order is complete" -- WRONG. Closes the loop.
> "Wait! Your order is not yet complete." -- RIGHT. Keeps the loop open.

## On Urgency
> "Fake urgency will backfire on you, and you'll lose all credibility."

## On Stories
> "When you stop teaching facts and start teaching through parables, your messages will stay with an audience longer."

> "You ALWAYS start any good story at the point of high drama." -- Daegan Smith

## On Seven vs. Eight Figures
> "The real difference between having a six-, seven-, or even eight-figure business is whether you understand the phases of a funnel and can successfully monetize the different points along the line."

## On Reverse Engineering
> "I never start to build out any new sales funnel" without finding "other people who already have a successful funnel."

> "Never move forward with two unknowns."

## On Being the Entrepreneur
> "Your job as the entrepreneur is to understand the strategy... and then hire the Facebook guy to run Facebook ads and the Google guy to run Google ads."
