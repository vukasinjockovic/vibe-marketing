# Referenced Marketers and Mentors
**Source:** DotCom Secrets (Russell Brunson, 2015)

## Primary Influences

### Dan Kennedy
- Direct marketing legend, Brunson's mentor for 6+ years
- Key quote used throughout: "Ultimately, the business that can spend the most to acquire a customer wins."
- Principle: "A buyer is a buyer is a buyer."
- Wrote the foreword for DotCom Secrets
- Ran the Titanium Mastermind ($25K) with Bill Glazer

### Mark Joyner
- Brunson's first Internet marketing mentor
- Key teaching: "Russell, you have to build a list."
- Concept: "Not all clicks are created equal" -- the pre-frame principle
- Built a huge company online

### Andre Chaperon
- Creator of the Soap Opera Sequence concept
- Builds sequences of 40-50 emails using narrative structure
- Brunson simplified it to the 5-email version

### Daegan Smith
- Taught Brunson to "ALWAYS start any good story at the point of high drama"
- Creator of the Invisible Funnel concept ("try before you buy")

### Armand Morin
- Taught Brunson "The Stack" closing technique
- One of the best stage presenters; explained the importance of introductions as pre-frames
- Went from 5-10% to 20%+ close rates after adopting The Stack

### Jeff Walker
- Made the Product Launch Funnel famous
- 4-video sequence: Wow and How, Transformational Education, Ownership Experience, The Offer

### Tony Robbins
- Brunson spoke at his Business Mastery seminar
- Endorsement quote on front of book
- Core principle cited: "If you want to be successful, find someone already doing what you want and model them."

## Other Referenced Figures

### Bill Glazer
- Partner with Dan Kennedy; ran Titanium Mastermind
- Brunson was their main Internet marketing trainer for 6 years

### John Alanis
- First person Brunson heard teach the Attractive Character concept
- Teaches men how to attract women; applied same principles to marketing

### Don Lapre
- Late-night infomercial marketer ("tiny little classified ads")
- Inspired 12-year-old Brunson's interest in direct response marketing

### Ryan Deiss & Perry Belcher
- Popularized the "tripwire" concept ($7 offers to get buyers in the door)

### David Frey
- Brunson's mentor on continuity: "If you don't have continuity, then you don't have a business."

### Todd Dickerson & Dylan Jones
- Co-created ClickFunnels software with Brunson
