# Key Quotes from The Boron Letters

Source: "The Boron Letters" by Gary Halbert (2013)

---

## On Markets and Strategy

> "The only advantage I want is A STARVING CROWD!"
-- Ch. 6

> "The very first thing you must come to realize is that you must become a 'student of markets.' Not products. Not techniques. Not copywriting."
-- Ch. 5

> "Sell People What They Want To Buy!"
-- Ch. 7

> "The mistake is finding or developing a product FIRST and then looking for a market to sell it to. This is backasswards."
-- Ch. 22

> "A guy with a new product cannot always find a hot market for that product but a guy who has uncovered a HOT MARKET can always find a product to fill the needs of that market."
-- Ch. 22

> "Products are a dime a dozen. They are important, but much less crucial to success than finding a hot market."
-- Ch. 22

## On Understanding Buyers

> "The way to deduce what people want to buy is to simply observe what they DO buy!"
-- Ch. 5

> "Be careful. You want to know what people actually DO buy, not what they SAY they buy."
-- Ch. 5

> "Truth can be determined NOT by how people use their mouths, but rather, how they use their wallets."
-- Ch. 8

> "Be skeptical of what people say. Be skeptical of surveys. Of questionnaires. Instead, believe in numbers."
-- Ch. 8

> "People don't always put their money where their mouths are; but they do nearly always put out their money where their true desires are."
-- Ch. 7

## On Lists and Targeting

> "Of the three guidelines, RECENCY is, by far, the most important of all."
-- Ch. 7

> "THE BEST LIST OF ALL IS YOUR OWN CUSTOMER LIST!"
-- Ch. 6

> "A lot of money can be made by making offers to people who are at an emotional turning point in their lives."
-- Ch. 8

## On Envelopes and First Contact

> "Everybody always opens all of their 'A' pile mail."
-- Ch. 11

> "If only half as many people open your envelope, only half as many even have a CHANCE to order!"
-- Ch. 11

## On Writing Copy

> "If you are writing for applause... you will go home with empty pockets!"
-- Ch. 17

> "Write for money!"
-- Ch. 17

> "The very best writing goes unnoticed."
-- Ch. 17

> "What happens when you actually write out a good ad in your own handwriting is that the words, the flow, the sentence structure, the sequence of information, and everything else about the writing of that ad becomes a part of you."
-- Ch. 17

> "The Best Way To Become A Good Writer... Is By... Writing Good Writing!"
-- Ch. 17

> "As a general rule, the more 'custom tailored' your promotion is, the more successful it will be."
-- Ch. 9

> "The words 'dramatically increase your sales volume' do not even begin to conjure up the visual imagery of 'a bushel of money.'"
-- Ch. 21

## On Selling

> "YOU CAN DO A BETTER SELLING JOB WHEN AT FIRST IT DOES NOT APPEAR YOU ARE ATTEMPTING TO DO A SALES JOB."
-- Ch. 18

> "In most publications, the editorial content gets 5 times as much readership as the advertising content."
-- Ch. 18

> "Tell him what he will get if he hurries and tell him what he will lose if he delays."
-- Ch. 16

> "You must always do even the obvious."
-- Ch. 16

## On Believability

> "When you say, in effect, 'Have I got a deal for you!' you need an explanation as to why you are offering this good deal."
-- Ch. 21

## On Attention

> "Your 'attention grabber' should be relevant. It should tie in with your message. It should make sense."
-- Ch. 14

> "A cheap shot. People resent this kind of fraud. Don't do it."
-- Ch. 14

## On First Impressions and Layout

> "I believe the 'sale' or 'no sale' decision is largely made the instant a prospect sees your ad and reads your headline."
-- Ch. 19

> "We unconsciously spend the rest of our so-called decision-making time not really making a decision after all, but instead, searching for justification for the decision we have already made."
-- Ch. 19

> "The Layout Of Your Advertisement Should Catch The Attention Of Your Reader... But... Not In A Way That Causes Him To 'Notice' The Layout!"
-- Ch. 18

## On Persistence and Mindset

> "Most of the world's work is done by people who didn't feel much like getting out of bed."
-- Ch. 8

> "You Don't Have To Get It Right... You Just Have To Get It MOVING!"
-- Ch. 20

> "When you get stuck or emotionally jammed up one of the ways to get yourself unclogged and flowing again is just to keep moving."
-- Ch. 10

> "The key is movement!"
-- Ch. 10

> "Money, in my opinion, especially big money, is most often a by-product of enthusiasm."
-- Ch. 5

> "HALT stands for hungry, angry, lonely, and tired, and you should never make a decision when you are any of those things."
-- Ch. 20

> "How You Feel Affects How You Think."
-- Ch. 1 (Bond's update)

> "I Work Like Crazy!"
-- Ch. 23

> "Nothing is impossible for a man who refuses to listen to reason."
-- Gary Halbert's favorite saying, Ch. 2 (Bond's update)

-- Ch. 4
