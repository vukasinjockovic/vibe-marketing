# Referenced Figures in The Boron Letters

## Copywriters and Marketers

### Robert Collier
- Author of "The Robert Collier Letter Book"
- Halbert instructs Bond to read it twice and take notes
- Source of the famous "Dollar Bill Letter" for a children's hospital (90%+ response rate)

### Claude Hopkins
- Author of "Scientific Advertising"
- Halbert instructs Bond to read it three times
- One of the foundational texts of direct response advertising

### Ben Suarez
- Author of "SuperBiz"
- Halbert recommends studying the headlines in his book
- Wrote "The Original Astrology Today Ad" -- one of the killer promotions Bond should hand-copy

### Old Aesop Glimm
- Pen name of a writer for "Printers Ink" magazine
- Wrote about how to improve DM and MO advertising
- Cited the "A Submarine That Flies?" anti-example of irrelevant attention grabbing

## Authors and Public Figures

### Arthur Haley
- Author of "Roots" (one of the bestsellers of all time)
- Halbert's example of internalizing craft: Haley copied great books by hand and traveled on rusty freighters to know what ocean crossings felt like
- (Note: Halbert writes "Arthur Haley" but likely means Alex Haley)

### Kurt Vonnegut
- Author of "Breakfast of Champions"
- Halbert admires his clarity and his frequent use of "And so on"
- "I did not say it was written by a sane person"

### Paul Bragg

### Paulo Airola
