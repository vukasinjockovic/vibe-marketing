# Gary C. Halbert (1938-2007)

Source: The Boron Letters
Role: Author, protagonist, teacher

## Who He Was

One of the most successful direct response copywriters in history. Called "The Prince of Print" and often considered the greatest copywriter who ever lived. His promotions generated sales measurable in the billions.

## Notable Works
- The Coat-of-Arms Letter: 7,156,000 cash-with-order customers; one of the most widely mailed sales letters ever
- The coat-of-arms follow-up letter: $40 million in sales
- "How To Collect From Social Security At Any Age": $800,000 from a single report
- The Japanese Penny promotion
- The Tova facelift ad
- The Gary Halbert Letter (newsletter)

## Context of The Boron Letters
Written from Boron Federal Prison Camp in California during a 10-month sentence in 1984. The letters were written to his youngest son Bond as a crash course in direct marketing and life advice.

## Core Philosophy
- Market first, product second
- Observe behavior, not stated preferences
- The starving crowd is the only advantage that matters
- Write for money, not applause
- The best writing is invisible
- Movement cures everything: creative blocks, emotional pain, indecision
- Physical fitness directly affects thinking quality

## Key Personal Traits
- Maintained extreme discipline even in prison (daily road work, fasting, writing)
- Controlled clients with an iron fist regarding copy decisions
- Could cut anyone from his life who was dead weight
- Believed enthusiasm was the #1 predictor of success
