# Joseph Sugarman
**Type:** researcher
**Status:** solid
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-10
**Aliases:** Joe Sugarman, JS&A

## Summary
American direct response copywriter, entrepreneur, and seminar leader. Founded JS&A Group, Inc. in the basement of his home, growing it into "America's largest single source of space-age products." Pioneered long-form print advertising for consumer electronics in the 1970s-80s, later moved to TV infomercials and home shopping (QVC). Created the BluBlocker sunglasses brand (20 million pairs sold). Conducted 17 exclusive copywriting seminars (1977-2000) at $2,000-$3,000 per attendee, training 312 students including founders of Cincinnati Microwave, Victoria's Secret marketing team, and many successful entrepreneurs.

## Note
This researcher is the author of The Adweek Copywriting Handbook in our collection. His frameworks and concepts are documented separately.

## Key Contributions
1. Developed the "Slippery Slide" framework for copy structure
2. Codified 31 Psychological Triggers for buying behavior
3. Pioneered telling stories in direct response advertising
4. Proved through A/B testing the power of satisfaction conviction and simplicity
5. Created the concept of "seeds of curiosity" for maintaining reading momentum
6. Built multiple businesses (JS&A, Consumers Hero, Battram Galleries, BluBlocker) from the power of copywriting alone
