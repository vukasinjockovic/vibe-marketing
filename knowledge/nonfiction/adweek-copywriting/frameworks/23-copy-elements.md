# The 23 Copy Elements
**Type:** framework
**Status:** solid
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-10
**Aliases:** Copy Elements Checklist, 23 Elements

## Summary
A comprehensive checklist of elements that should be considered for every piece of copy, presented in Chapter 18. Each element represents a component or technique that the copywriter should deliberately address. They serve as both a construction guide (what to include) and a quality check (what to verify).

## The 23 Elements
1. Typeface
2. First Sentence
3. Second Sentence
4. Paragraph Headings
5. Product Explanation
6. New Features
7. Technical Explanation
8. Anticipate Objections
9. Resolve Objections
10. Gender
11. Clarity
12. Cliches (avoid them)
13. Rhythm
14. Service
15. Physical Facts
16. Trial Period
17. Price Comparison
18. Testimonials
19. Price
20. Offer Summary
21. Avoid Saying Too Much
22. Ease of Ordering
23. Ask for the Order

## Key Findings
1. Technical explanation builds authority and credibility, even for simple products -- Schultz used it for grapefruit.
   -- Source: Adweek Copywriting Handbook, Ch.18

2. Physical facts (weight, dimensions, limits) must be complete -- "Give readers any excuse not to buy and they won't buy."
   -- Source: Adweek Copywriting Handbook, Ch.18

3. "Ask for the Order" must always appear explicitly. Never assume the reader knows what to do.
   -- Source: Adweek Copywriting Handbook, Ch.18
