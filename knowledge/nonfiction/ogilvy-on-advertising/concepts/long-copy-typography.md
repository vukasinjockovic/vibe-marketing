# Long Copy Typography Boosters
**Type:** concept
**Status:** solid
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-10
**Aliases:** typography rules, copy readership boosters, typographic techniques

## Summary
Ogilvy's collected set of typographic techniques, each backed by research, that increase readership of long-copy advertisements. These are precise, measurable interventions: drop-initials increase readership 13%, leading between paragraphs increases readership 12%, headlines below illustrations are read by 10% more people, quoted headlines increase recall 28%, and news headlines increase recall 22%.

## Key Findings
1. Drop-initials increase readership by 13%.
   -- Source: Ogilvy on Advertising, Ch.7

2. Leading (line-spacing) between paragraphs increases readership by 12%.
   -- Source: Ogilvy on Advertising, Ch.7

3. Headlines below illustrations are read by 10% more people than headlines above.
   -- Source: Ogilvy on Advertising, Ch.7

4. Quoted headlines increase recall by 28%.
   -- Source: Ogilvy on Advertising, Ch.7

5. News headlines increase recall by 22%.
   -- Source: Ogilvy on Advertising, Ch.7

6. Reverse type (white on black) cuts readership dramatically; one charity raised twice as much with black on white.
   -- Source: Ogilvy on Advertising, Ch.7

## Key Quotes
> "If you start your body copy with a drop-initial, you increase readership by an average of 13 per cent."

> "If you use leading (line-spacing) between paragraphs, you increase readership by an average of 12 per cent."
