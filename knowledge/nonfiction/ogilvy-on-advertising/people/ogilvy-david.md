# David Ogilvy
**Type:** researcher
**Status:** solid
**Last Updated:** 2026-02-10
**Aliases:** David Ogilvy, Ogilvy

## Summary
Founder of Ogilvy & Mather (one of the four biggest advertising agencies in the world), author of Confessions of an Advertising Man (1963) and Ogilvy on Advertising (1983). Before advertising, he was a chef in Paris, an Aga cooking stove salesman in Scotland (door-to-door), a researcher for George Gallup at Princeton, a farmer among the Amish in Pennsylvania, and an intelligence officer with British Intelligence during WWII. He bridged Hopkins' "hard sell" research tradition with creative excellence, and is credited with popularizing the concept of brand images in 1953.

## Note
This researcher is the author of Ogilvy on Advertising in our collection. His frameworks and concepts are documented separately.

## Key Findings
1. Started Ogilvy & Mather in 1949; grew from "a creative boutique in New York" to one of the four biggest agencies with 140 offices in 40 countries.
   -- Source: Ogilvy on Advertising, Ch.1

2. Career path: chef, door-to-door salesman, Gallup researcher, farmer, intelligence officer, then advertising.
   -- Source: Ogilvy on Advertising, Ch.1, Ch.3, Ch.18

3. Rubicam once told him: "You are Claude Hopkins with a college education."
   -- Source: Ogilvy on Advertising, Ch.18
